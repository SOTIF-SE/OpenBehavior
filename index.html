OpenBehavior: A Behavior-Centric Language for Autonomous Driving Scenario Description and Generation

This page presents multiple categories of bugs discovered in the Apollo autonomous driving system.

Bug Categories:
R1-T At a T-junction, the ego vehicle proceeds straight through the intersection. One NPC vehicle travels on the same road as the ego vehicle, while three NPC vehicles approach from the road on the left. All vehicles have destinations on the same road, but at different locations.
R1-T1 Apollo collides with the adversarial vehicle following its lane change.
R1-T2 Due to Apollo’s failure to yield, it collides with the second left-turning vehicle.
R1-T3 Following a sudden lane change by the adversarial vehicle, Apollo makes a sharp turn and collides with a left-turning vehicle.
R1-T4 Due to Apollo’s failure to yield, it collides with the third left-turning vehicle.
R1-T5 After passing the intersection, Apollo fails to recognize the slow-moving vehicle ahead, resulting in a rear-end collision.
R1-X At a four-way intersection, the ego vehicle proceeds straight through the intersection. One NPC vehicle is in the lane to the left of the ego vehicle, while three NPC vehicles are on the road to the right of the ego vehicle. Among these NPC vehicles, three have their destinations on the road to the left of the ego vehicle, and one has its destination on the same road as the ego vehicle but at a different location.
R1-X1 Influenced by the adversarial vehicle, Apollo fails to yield at the intersection and collides with the first vehicle approaching from the right.
R1-X2 Influenced by the adversarial vehicle, Apollo fails to yield at the intersection, resulting in a side-impact collision with the first vehicle on the right.
R1-X3 Apollo crosses the stop line and stops in the middle of the intersection, causing a collision with a vehicle approaching from the right.
R1-L On a highway, the ego vehicle and four NPC vehicles are on the same road. The NPC vehicles have randomly assigned positions and destinations, all of which remain on the same road.
R1-L1 The adversarial vehicle cuts in, and Apollo fails to react and brake in time, resulting in a collision.
R1-L2 An NPC vehicle changes lanes, and Apollo fails to react in time, leading to a collision.
R1-L3 The vehicle ahead slows down due to obstruction by an adversarial vehicle. Apollo fails to recognize the reduced speed and collides with it.
R1-L4 Apollo fails to detect a vehicle approaching from the left rear and changes lanes to the left, resulting in a collision.
R1-L5 Despite a vehicle ahead, Apollo plans a leftward maneuver and passes extremely close to an NPC vehicle.
R1-L6 Apollo fails to yield to a diagonally stopped roadside vehicle intending to change lanes, leading to a collision.
R1-L7 Apollo fails to detect a traffic accident ahead and does not brake, resulting in a rear-end collision.
R1-L8 Apollo changes lanes to the right and collides with a normally driving vehicle on the right.
R1-L9 Apollo initiates a lane change, attempts to abort midway due to a vehicle ahead, but reacts too late and collides with the front vehicle.
R1-L10 Apollo cuts in and collides with a vehicle approaching from the right rear.
R1-L11 Despite a vehicle on its left, Apollo proceeds with a left lane change, resulting in a side-impact collision with an NPC vehicle.
R1-L12 A vehicle ahead hesitates during a lane change and comes to a stop, but Apollo continues forward and passes dangerously close to the NPC vehicle.
R1-L13 Apollo slows down and brakes due to uncertainty about the adversarial vehicle ahead, and is rear-ended by a following vehicle.
R1-L14 The vehicle ahead moves slowly, but Apollo fails to detect this and brake in time, resulting in a collision.
R2 In multiple scenarios, the ego vehicle fails to reach its destination.
R2-1 Traffic congestion prevents Apollo from reaching its destination.
R2-2 Under the influence of an NPC vehicle, Apollo loses control of the vehicle.
R2-3 Two NPC vehicles merge ahead, preventing Apollo from proceeding.
